The Gordie Howe Bridge Project

Home / Feature / The Gordie Howe Bridge Project

  • Boreholes up to 150 feet in depth
  • Bedrock artesian conditions while drilling
  • Installation of monitoring wells in artesian conditions
  • Proper methods and equipment to control and prevent flows.

Portland cement and heavy drill mud were used to control, stop, and prevent future flows while drilling and installing monitoring wells. The artesian conditions were encountered in the bedrock at roughly 120 feet. The drill casing was lightly cemented into the rock formation, to allow bedrock coring to proceed while controlling the artesian flow. The boreholes were drilled deeper, backfilled, and installed with instrumentation. Once all boreholes were sealed properly and in accordance with Regulation 903, the drill casing was removed.

See picture below for surface casing, which our client could relieve the water pressure and conduct a packer test inside the well if needed. A pressure gauge was installed to show the pressure inside the well before opening the well.

The Gordie Howe International Bridge project is a once-in-a-generation undertaking. Not only will the project deliver much-needed transportation improvements for international travellers, it will also provide jobs and opportunities for growth to the Windsor-Detroit region and includes features that make this project truly distinctive.
